The chart displays carbon emissions by sector for various countries and geographical regions from 1990 to 2021, highlighting contributions to global greenhouse gas emissions by activities such as agriculture, land use changes, waste management, buildings, industry, transportation, electricity and heat generation, and other fuel combustion.

This data provides critical insights into the distribution of emissions across different economic activities and regions, helping to identify the primary drivers of climate change. Understanding these patterns is essential for developing targeted policies and strategies to mitigate emissions, transition to sustainable practices, and address global warming.With climate change posing significant risks to ecosystems, economies, and human health, this data underscores the urgency of coordinated global efforts to reduce carbon emissions and limit temperature rise to internationally agreed targets.

The line chart provides a visual representation of trends over time. It highlights significant changes and patterns in the data series.

The pie chart is used to depict proportions and percentages, offering a clear view of the relative size of each category in the dataset.

The radar chart compares multiple variables on a two-dimensional plane, emphasizing the differences and similarities between data points.
